Uluwatu Temple

Perched on a cliff 70 meters above the Indian Ocean, Uluwatu Temple is one of Bali's most iconic sea temples. It's famous for stunning sunsets, dramatic views, and the traditional Kecak dance performance held every evening.

Tegallalang Rice Terraces

Located in Ubud, these beautiful rice terraces showcase Bali's traditional subak irrigation system. Visitors can enjoy scenic views, take a swing over the terraces, or learn about local farming practices.

Tanah Lot Temple

A unique sea temple built on a rock formation, Tanah Lot is one of Bali's most photographed landmarks. It's especially beautiful during sunset when the temple silhouette creates a magical scene.

Ubud Monkey Forest

A sacred nature reserve and temple complex, this forest in Ubud is home to hundreds of playful long-tailed macaques. Visitors can explore the lush greenery, ancient temples, and statues.

Seminyak Beach

Known for its upscale vibe, Seminyak Beach offers golden sands, luxury resorts, trendy beach clubs, and vibrant nightlife. It's a perfect spot for relaxing, surfing, or enjoying Bali's stunning sunsets.

Mount Batur

This active volcano is a popular destination for sunrise hikes. Visitors can trek to the summit for breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape, including Lake Batur and nearby villages.
